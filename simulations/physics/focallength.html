Find the focal length of a convex lens by parallax method

THEORY

The parallax method is a common technique used to find the focal length of a convex lens. It involves observing the apparent shift in the position of an object when viewed through the lens from two different angles. The theory behind this method relies on the basic principles of optics and the behavior of light rays passing through a convex lens using the parallax method.

Principle of convex lens:

A convex lens is a transparent optical element that converges light rays that pass through it. Light rays that are parallel to the principal axis of the lens converge at a point known as the focal point(F). The distance between the lens and the focal point is called the focal length (f) of the lens. The focal length can be calculated using the formula: 1/f=1/p+1/q Where,
f= focal length of the lens
p=Distance between the lens and the object.
q=Distance between the lens and the image,

Note

According to sign-convention, p has a negative value and q has a positive value for the convex lens. Hence, f comes positive.

EXPERIMENTAL ARRANGEMENT:

FOCAL LENGTH OF A CONVEX LENS:

MATERIALS REQUIRED:

  1. Convex lens installed in a lens holder with base.
  2. Two needles upright.
  3. Optical bench
  4. Piece of paper(object).

PROCEDURE

  • Get an image of a distant object through the given convex lens on the opposite walls of the laboratory
  • Measure the distance between the lens and the image, which is the approximate focal length.
  • Find the index correction between the lens and the object needle.
  • Place the lens upright in the holder of the optical bench in such a way that one needle upright should be on the left side of the lens and the other on the right side of the lens.
  • Place a piece of paper on one side of the needle upright. This upright will be considered as the objective needle O and the other will be image I.
  • The distance between two needles upright should be greater than four times the approximate focal length of the given lens.
  • Now, remove the parallax between the image of the object and the image needle.
  • Note down the position of the object needle, lens, and image needle in a table.
  • For more reading move the object needle slightly and again remove the parallax between the image of the object needle and the image needle keeping the lens position fixed and note down the positions of the object needle, image needle, and lens
  • In this way take at least five readings.

    • PRECAUTIONS

  • Ensure that the lens is clean and free from any defects.
  • Set up the apparatus on a stable surface to avoid any vibrations or accidental movements.
  • If using a lamp as a light source, ensure that it does not generate excessive heat that could affect the lens or the image formed.
  • Ensure that the lens is centered in its mount or stand. Any displacement from the central position could lead to incorrect focal length measurements.
  • Parallax should be removed from tip to tip by keeping an eye at a distance at least 30 cm. away from the needle
  • The object needle should be placed at such a distance that only real, inverted image of it
