Demonstration of the presence of tar in cigarette smoke and also showing by charts and pictures the difference of lungs of smokers and non-smokers

Cigarette smoke contains various harmful substances, including tar, which is a sticky, brown residue that forms when tobacco is burned. Tar contains numerous toxic chemicals and carcinogens that can have detrimental effects on the respiratory system. By conducting a practical demonstration, we can show the presence of tar in cigarette smoke and visually illustrate the differences in lung health between smokers and non-smokers.

Apparatus

  1. Cigarettes or tobacco samples
  2. Glass jar with a lid
  3. Cotton balls or filter paper
  4. Safety goggles and gloves
  5. Spirit lamp
  6. Pictures or illustrations of healthy and diseased lungs

Precautions

It is important to conduct this practical demonstration in a well-ventilated area and follow safety protocols to minimize exposure to harmful smoke and protect the well-being of participants.

Difference between healthy lungs and diseased lungs:

  1. Healthy lungs exhibit a pinkish color and smooth texture.
  2. Diseased lungs associated with smoking may show tar deposits, blackened areas, and indications of respiratory diseases such as lung cancer or COPD.
  3. The comparison between healthy and diseased lungs visually emphasizes the negative impact of smoking on lung health.

Observation

  1. Presence of tar in cigarette smoke:
    • The cotton ball or filter paper inside the jar turns brown due to the condensation of tar.
    • Note the presence of tar by rubbing white tissue paper on the inner wall of the funnel.

Procedure

  1. Demonstrating the presence of tar in cigarette smoke
  2. Put on safety goggles and gloves to ensure personal safety.
  3. Take a glass jar and place a cotton ball or a piece of filter paper at the bottom.
  4. Ignite a cigarette using match and let it burn for a few seconds.
  5. Ignite a cigarette using match and let it burn for a few seconds.
  6. Blow the smoke into the jar and quickly close the lid to trap the smoke inside.
  7. Allow the jar to sit undisturbed for a few minutes to allow the tar to condense on the cotton ball or filter paper.
  8. Carefully remove the cotton ball or filter paper and observe the brown discoloration caused by the tar.
  9. This demonstrates the presence of tar in cigarette smoke.

Steps

  3. Add 2-3 drops of sugar solution of 0.1; 0.9 and 5% on slide X, Y and Z respectively.
