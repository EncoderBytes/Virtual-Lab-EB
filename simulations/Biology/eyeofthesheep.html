Identify the different parts and draw a labeled diagram of the longitudinal section of the eye of a sheep or a bull

Objective

The objective of this practical is to identify and understand the different parts of a sheep eye by examining a longitudinal section model.

Materials

  1. Sheep eye model
  2. Disposable gloves
  3. Hand lens or magnifying glass
  4. Paper, pen, and colored pencils for drawing the labelled diagram

Precautions

  1. Wear disposable gloves throughout the practical to maintain hygiene and prevent the spread of bacteria or contaminants.
  2. Ensure students are supervised throughout the practical to prevent any unsafe behaviors

Procedure

  1. Show the sheep eye model to the students, pointing out the different parts such as the cornea, iris, lens, retina, and optic nerve.

  2. Observation of External Structures: Examine the external features of the sheep eye model. b. Observe the cornea, which is the transparent front part of the eye, covering the iris and the pupil. c. Identify the iris, the colored part of the eye, which controls the size of the pupil. d. Note the pupil, the dark circular opening in the center of the iris.

  3. Examination of Internal Structures Identify and observe the lens, which is a clear structure located behind the iris. Note its shape and transparency. c. Observe the retina, a thin layer of tissue at the back of the eye that contains photoreceptor cells for vision. d. Locate the optic nerve, a bundle of nerve fibers that carries visual information from the retina to the brain. e. Examine other internal structures, such as the aqueous humor and vitreous humor, which help maintain the shape of the eye.

Steps

  1. Choose three slides and mark them X, Y and Z respectively.
  2. Put a drop of blood on each slide by piercing the tip of the finger by a sterilized needle.
  3. Add 2-3 drops of sugar solution of 0.1; 0.9 and 5% on slide X, Y and Z respectively.
  4. Cover each slide with a cover slip and observe under microscope

1. What is the purpose of examining the longitudinal section of the eye?

2. Name the transparent structure at the front of the eye that allows light to enter.

3. Which part of the eye controls the amount of light entering the eye?

4. What is the function of the lens in the eye?

5. Which structure of the eye is responsible for converting light into neural signals?

6. What is the name of the area on the retina that contains a high concentration of photoreceptor cells?

7. Which part of the eye is responsible for producing aqueous humor?

8. Name the structure that connects the eye to the brain, allowing the transmission of visual signals.

9. What is the function of the vitreous humor in the eye?
