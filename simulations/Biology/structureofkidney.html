Examination of the structure of the Kidney (Sheep Kidney / Model)

The objective of this practical is to examine and understand the structure of the kidney using a model. By observing the various components of the kidney model, students will gain a better understanding of its anatomical features and functions.

Materials

  1. Kidney model (available in biology labs or educational supply stores)
  2. Dissection tools (scalpel or scissors)
  3. Disposable gloves
  4. Hand lens or magnifying glass

Precautions

  1. Wear disposable gloves to prevent the spread of bacteria or contaminants.
  2. Follow safety guidelines including rules regarding the use of dissection tools, proper disposal of materials, and handling of chemicals.
  3. Clean and disinfect After completing the practical.
  4. Ensure that students are supervised throughout the practical.

Procedure

  1. Introduction Begin the practical by explaining the importance of the kidney in the human body, highlighting its role in filtering waste products and maintaining fluid balance. b. Show the kidney model to the students, pointing out the different structures such as the renal cortex, renal medulla, renal pelvis, and renal blood vessels.

  2. External Anatomy Examine the external features of the kidney model. b. Observe the overall shape of the kidney, noting its bean-like appearance. c. Identify the hilum, a concave region on the medial side of the kidney model. Explain that it is the point of entry and exit for blood vessels, nerves, and the ureter. d. Discuss the protective layer surrounding the kidney called the renal capsule.

  3. Internal Anatomy Use dissection tools to carefully cut the kidney model along the longitudinal axis. b. Identify and observe the renal cortex, which is the outer layer of the kidney. Note its granular appearance. c. Beneath the cortex, locate the renal medulla, which consists of triangular structures called renal pyramids. Explain that the medulla contains tubules and blood vessels. d. Locate the renal pelvis, a funnel-shaped structure at the center of the kidney model. Explain that it collects urine produced by the kidney and directs it to the ureter. e. Observe the renal blood vessels, such as the renal artery and renal vein, within the kidney model.
  4. Nephron Structure Using a hand lens or magnifying glass, examine the cut surface of the kidney model to observe the microscopic structures called nephrons. b. Point out the glomerulus, a network of tiny blood vessels, and the renal tubules connected to it. c. Explain that the nephron is the functional unit of the kidney responsible for filtering blood and producing urine.

Steps

  1. Choose three slides and mark them X, Y and Z respectively.
  2. Put a drop of blood on each slide by piercing the tip of the finger by a sterilized needle.
  3. Add 2-3 drops of sugar solution of 0.1; 0.9 and 5% on slide X, Y and Z respectively.
  4. Cover each slide with a cover slip and observe under microscope

1. What is the purpose of examining the structure of the kidney?

2. What is the main function of the kidney?

3. Which part of the kidney is responsible for the filtration of blood?

4. What is the functional unit of the kidney?

5. Which structure of the kidney is responsible for collecting urine from the nephrons?

6. What is the role of the glomerulus in the kidney?

7. Where does the reabsorption of water and nutrients occur in the kidney?

8. Which part of the nephron is responsible for the reabsorption of water and electrolytes?

9. What is the function of the renal pelvis?

10. What are some common kidney disorders?
