Identification of cell wall, cell membrane, cytoplasm, nucleus and chloroplast using microscope (Alovera)

Theory

The aim of this practical is to determine the presence and location of the cell wall, cell membrane, cytoplasm, and nucleus in Aloe Vera leaf cells using specific indicators.

Materials:

  1. Aloe Vera leaf
  2. Microscope
  3. Glass slides
  4. Coverslips
  5. Eosin solution
  6. Distilled water
  7. Dropper
  8. Microscope slides
  9. Microscope coverslips
  10. Microscope

Observations

The presence of a distinct cell wall, which appears as a dark stained layer surrounding the cell.

  • The presence of a cell membrane, which appears as a thin, transparent layer just inside the cell wall.
  • The presence of cytoplasm, which is stained by safranin and fills the cell.
  • The presence of a nucleus, which appears as a darkly stained structure within the cytoplasm.

Procedure

  1. Take a small section of an Aloe Vera leaf using a clean scalpel or razor blade.
  2. Gently scrape the inner part of the leaf to obtain a thin layer of cells.
  3. Place the scraped cells onto a clean glass slide.
  4. Apply a few drops of eosin solution onto the Aloe Vera leaf cells.
  5. Eosin stains the cell wall, making it more visible under the microscope.
  6. Leave the iodine solution on the slide for about 2 minutes.
  7. Carefully rinse off the excess iodine solution with distilled water.
  8. Gently blot the slide to remove excess water.
  9. Place a drop of eosin stain onto the Aloe Vera leaf cells. Indicator stains the cytoplasm and nucleus.
  10. Carefully lower a coverslip onto the stained leaf cells, ensuring there are no air bubbles trapped.
  11. Place the prepared slide on the microscope stage. Start with the lowest magnification objective lens (e.g., 10x) and bring the cells into focus using the coarse and fine adjustment knobs.
  12. Observe the cells under the microscope and focus on a single cell.

Precautions:

  • Handle the microscope and glassware with care to avoid breakage.
  • Avoid direct contact with the stains and indicators. If contact occurs, rinse with water immediately.
  • Use a dropper or pipette to handle liquids and avoid mouth pipetting.

IDENTIFICATION OF CELL WALL, CELL MEMBRANE, CYTOPLASM, NUCLEUS AND CHLOROPLAST USING microscope (ALOVERA)

Steps

  1. Cut a thin slice of aloe vera with scalpel.
  2. Place it on clean glass slide.
  3. Pour a drop of water and then eosin solution on the scrap.
  4. Place cover slip on it.
  5. Place it under the microscope.
  6. Now observe features.

1. What is the purpose of staining the plant cells in the determination of cell wall, cell membrane, cytoplasm, and nucleus?

2. Which indicator is used to stain the cell wall in plant cells?

3. Which structure of the plant cell is responsible for providing support and protection?

4. What does the cell membrane of a plant cell do?

5. Which stain is used to stain the cytoplasm and nucleus in plant cells?

6. Which part of the plant cell is responsible for carrying out various metabolic activities?

7. What is the function of the nucleus in a plant cell?

8. Which component of the plant cell is stained by iodine solution?

9. Which of the following is NOT a characteristic of the cell wall in plant cells?

10. Which part of the plant cell is responsible for maintaining turgidity and preventing excessive water uptake?
