Separate the given mixture of alcohol and water by distillation

The Theory

A fractional distillation technique can be used to separate a mixture of alcohol (ethanol) and water. This approach is based on the fact that the boiling points of the compounds in the mixture differ. "Fractional" distillation is used to separate mixtures of two liquids having differing boiling points, such as alcohol and water.

Real Lab Procedure

  1. Fill the round-bottom flask with the ethanol-water mixture, and then assemble the fractional distillation apparatus by connecting the fractionating column to the flask.
  2. Connect the condenser to the fractioning column and place the distillate-capturing flask beneath it to capture the distillate.
  3. Using the Spirit lamp under the round-bottom flask, heat the mixture to above the boiling point of ethanol (80oC).
  4. Maintain a constant temperature in the mixture until the boiling stops. At this moment the distillation process is complete at all.
