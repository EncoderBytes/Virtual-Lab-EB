Prepare pure copper sulphate crystal from the given impure sample

Theory

Water is used to shake the given sample. The copper sulphate in the sample dissolves while the insoluble contaminants remain. The solution is filtered. The filtrate is concentrated and cooled to crystallisation temperature. Crystals of Copper Sulphate (CuSO4.5H2O) separate while cooling.

Procedure

  1. Solution Preparation

    Take around 25-30 mL of water and add small amounts of powdered crude copper sulphate to it. To dissolve it, give it a good stir. Make multiple additions of the powdered sample until just a small amount remains undissolved after some stirring.

  2. Filtration of the Solution and Concentration of the Filtrate to Crystallisation point.

    Filter the solution and collect the filtrate in a china contaminants are left on the filter paper as residue. To avoid crust formation on the dish's side, the solution is vigorously swirled as it heats up with a glass rod. If a crust forms, it is removed with a glass rod and dissolves into the solution. Allow the solution in the dish not to boil. Cool a drop of the solution by blowing it off the end of a glass rod. When a crust or tiny crystals emerge on the glass rod, this indicates that the crystallisation point has been reached. Turn off the burner and turn off the heat. Place a crystallising dish over the hot saturated solution.

  3. Drying and crystal separation.

    Carefully decant the mother liquor. Remove the crystals using a filter paper that has been soaked in the solution. Transfer the crystals to another filter paper and gently press them between the folds of the filter papers to dry.
