DETERMINE THE EXACT MOLARITY OF THE Na2CO3 SOLUTION VOLUMETRICALLY

Chemical analysis is divided into two main classes:

  1. Quantitative Analysis: The object of quantitative analysis is to determine the actual amounts of the constituents of a compound, and the amount of material dissolved in solutions.
  2. Qualitative Analysis: This type of analysis involves the investigation and identification of substances in their simplest or complicated forms. Sodium carbonate (Na2CO3) is available as washing soda (Na2CO3.10H2O) and soda ash (Na2CO3) also called anhydrous sodium When plants are dried and carbonate. The aqueous solution of sodium carbonate is alkaline in nature.

Apparatus Required:

Burette, Conical Flask, Pipette, Funnel, Beakers, Dropper, Iron Stand

Chemicals Required:

  1. Standard solution of 0.1M HCl
  2. Solution of NaOH
  3. Distilled Water
  4. Methyl Orange as indicator
  1. Wash Pipette and Conical Flask with Distilled Water.
  2. Transfer 100cm3 of Na2CO3 solution with Pipette to Conical Flask
  3. Add 2-3 drops of Methyl Orange indicator to conical flask containing 100cm3 Na2CO3 solution, solution will turn Orange Color.
  4. Start adding HCl solution from Burette to Conical Flask drops wise.
  5. Continue adding 0.1M HCl until Orange color solution of Na2CO3 in Conical Flask become red and that is the end point of the titration.
  6. Note the amount of HCl used and make calculation for Molarity of Na2CO3 solution by using equation: M1V1=M2V2
