Identify saturated and unsaturated organic compounds by KMnO4 test

Saturated:

In chemistry, a saturated compound refers to a molecule that contains only single bonds between its atoms. Saturated hydrocarbons are organic compounds consisting of carbon and hydrogen atoms, where all carbon atoms are bonded to the maximum number of hydrogen atoms possible. Saturated fats are a type of dietary fat that predominantly come from animal sources and are solid at room temperature.

Unsaturated

In contrast, an unsaturated compound contains at least one double or triple bond between its atoms. Unsaturated hydrocarbons are organic compounds with double or triple bonds between carbon atoms. Unsaturated fats are primarily found in plant-based oils, as well as fatty fish and nuts. They remain liquid at room temperature. Picture an unsaturated compound as a partially filled house, where some bonding positions are vacant due to the presence of double or triple bonds.

Materials

Test-tube, test-tube rack, test tube holder, dropper, cinnamic acid solution, distilled water, KMnO4 solution

procedure

  1. Take about 10cm3 of cinnamic acid solution in a test tube.
  2. Take pink colour dilute solution of potassium per manganate in another test tube.
  3. Add to it pink colure dilute solution of potassium permanganate drop by drop.
  4. The pink solution becomes colourless.
  5. This shows that the given compound is unsaturated.

Results:

The given substance is unsaturated having carbon carbon double bond or triple bond that decolourize potassium per manganate solution.
