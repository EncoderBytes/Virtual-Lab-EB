Standardize the given NaOH solution volumetrically

Theory

Standardization:

is a process used to determine the exact concentration of a solution.

Titration:

Titration is a technique for determining the concentration of a certain chemical component in a given solution.

Standardization :

is the process of determining the exact concentration (molarity) of a solution.

Titration :

is one type of analytical procedure often used in standardization. In a titration, an exact volume of one substance is reacted with a known amount of another substance. The point at which the reaction is complete in a titration is referred to as the endpoint. A chemical substance known as an indicator is used to indicate (signal) the endpoint. The indicator used in this experiment is phenolphthalein. Phenolphthalein, an organic compound, is colorless in acidic solution and pink in basic solution. Sodium hydroxide is a deliquescent material (it absorbs moisture from the air). It is impossible to exactly weigh it. Sodium hydroxide is sometimes known as caustic soda or lye. It's a frequent ingredient in cleaning products and soaps. Sodium hydroxide is a white, odourless substance at ambient temperature. Sodium hydroxide liquid is colourless and odourless. It is extremely reactive to strong acids and water. Sodium hydroxide is a caustic substance. As it dissolves, NaOH can react with moisture in the air and generate heat. If it is near flammable materials, this heat can be enough to start a fire. In bar soaps and detergents, sodium hydroxide is employed. Sodium Hydroxide is also used to unblock pipes as a drain cleaner. Industry uses around 56% of the sodium hydroxide produced, with the paper industry using 25% of the NaOH produced. Other applications include fuel cell manufacturing, food curing, vegetable skin removal for canning, bleach, drain cleaner, oven cleaner, soaps, detergent, paper making, paper recycling, aluminum ore processing, oxide coating, cotton fabric processing, pickling, pain relievers, anticoagulants to prevent blood clots, cholesterol lowering medications, and water treatment.

Apparatus Required:

Burette, Conical Flask, Pipette, Funnel, Beakers, Dropper, Iron Stand

Chemicals Required:

  1. Standard solution of 0.1M HCl
  2. Solution of NaOH
  3. Distilled Water
  4. Phenolphthalein as indicator

procedure

  • Wash Pipette and Conical Flask with Distilled Water
  • Transfer 100cm3 of NaOH solution with Pipette to Conical Flask
  • Add 2-3 drops of Phenolphthalein indicator to conical flask containing 100cm3 NaOH solution, solution will turn pink.
  • Add 100cm3 of 0.1M HCl solution to Burette with the help of Funnel.
  • Start adding HCl solution from Burette to Conical Flask drops wise.
  • Continue adding 0.1M HCl until pink solution of NaOH in Conical Flask become colorless and that is the end point of the titration.
  • Note the amount of HCl used and make calculation for Molarity of NaOH solution by using equation:
    • M1V1=M2V2
